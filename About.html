The Virtual Reality Therapist

Since 2011, I've been focused on making mental health support more effective and accessible by combining hands-on therapy with virtual reality (VR) technology.
I’m passionate about using digital tools like VR to take insights from research and apply them directly to therapy, helping people in meaningful ways.
As one of the early VR adopters world-wide, I have over a decade of experience creating VR programs and working with individuals dealing with anxieties, social phobia and psychosis.

My path as a psychologist started back in 2007, specializing in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy.
I completed my PhD in 2021 with honors, where I focused on VR therapy for treating psychosis.
Since moving to Australia in 2021 to work as a Research Fellow at The University of Melbourne and Orygen, I’ve been co-creating VR therapies specifically for young people.
These therapies aim to build social skills, boost resilience, and reduce the impact of ongoing mental health challenges.
